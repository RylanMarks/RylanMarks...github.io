Article #1

Article #2

In this Blog Post I will go over a review about my favorite game: Titanfall 2. The great majority of reviews for this game are very positive. I would even say that 90% of reviews are 5 stars. The review I chose starts out by saying that Titanfall 2 is "An amazing shooter with lots of variety in both cosmetics and modes,with many weapons, titans, pilot abilities, pilot and gun colors/camo, maps, factions, banner cards and animations within combat" I would have to agree on this. There are so many different weapon, pilot, and titan skins. There are 7 different pilots you can use with their own abilities along with 7 different titans with their own kits as well. The review also described the movement in Titanfall 2. The game has movement mechanics never before seen in an FPS. The game allowed wallrunning and double jumping. The engine Titanfall 2 has also allowed the player to pretty much "fly" across the map with various movement techniques. I believe Titanfall 2 rightfully deserves its positive reviews.

Article #4

The game I will use for this blog post is called The Legend of Zelda: Breath of the Wild. This game is good because it has so many different possible things you can do. You can collect many different types of equipment such as weapons, clothing, materials, etc. There are also many different meals you can cook in this RPG, each with its own buff. There are also plenty of things to do and places to go. One of the main points of interest you will find throughout Hyrule are stables. You can stay a night there, sell/buy materials, and register horses to be your companion and source of transportation. There is so much more to this game that I did not mention because Nintendo worked their hearts out on it, and that's what makes a good RPG. A bad RPG would have a small map, little resources and gear, with a very linear storyline with not much to do outside of the main story.


Article #5

Toxicity in games has been around ever since the first online game, especially in competitive games, such as Call of Duty, League of Legends, and similar games. It has arguably been a growing problem in recent years, and only now is some action being taken against it, such as Xbox's new "8 strike system". The most effective way to censor yourself to it is to simply block, mute, and/or report the player expressing toxic behavior. Toxic behavior could be prevented more effectively in the future if large gaming companies monitored their in-game voice and text chat more and set punishments along with it.
